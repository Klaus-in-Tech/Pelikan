Frequently Asked Questions

Clearing and forwarding goods in Uganda can be a complex process, involving various regulations and procedures. To help navigate this process, we've compiled a list of frequently asked questions about the sector.

  • What is the process of clearing and forwarding goods in Uganda?

    The process of clearing and forwarding goods in Uganda involves several steps, including obtaining import/export permits, preparing documentation, paying customs duties and taxes, and arranging for transportation and delivery.

  • What are the major ports and airports in Uganda for clearing and forwarding?

    The major ports for clearing and forwarding in Uganda are the Port of Mombasa in Kenya and the Port of Dar es Salaam in Tanzania. The main airports for air freight are Entebbe International Airport and Kigali International Airport.

  • What documents are required for clearing and forwarding goods in Uganda?

    The required documents for clearing and forwarding goods in Uganda include the bill of lading, commercial invoice, packing list, certificate of origin, import/export permit, and any other relevant permits or licenses.

  • What is the role of a customs agent in clearing and forwarding goods in Uganda?

    A customs agent is a licensed professional who specializes in clearing goods through customs on behalf of importers and exporters. They are familiar with customs regulations and procedures and can help ensure that goods comply with all relevant laws and regulations.

  • What is the role of a freight forwarder in clearing and forwarding goods in Uganda?

    A freight forwarder is a company that specializes in arranging the transportation of goods on behalf of shippers. They handle all aspects of the shipment, including booking space on airlines or ships, preparing documentation, and arranging for customs clearance.
